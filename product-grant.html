Engage with the Technology Division and share your knowledge with others in the planning profession through this initiative made possible with the support of the 2024 American Planning Association's Product Development Grant. This grant will allow the Technology Division to develop a planning technology handbook and a refined version of the "Planning Through the Black Mirror" card game, which together aim to provide accessible, centralized guidance on technology strategy, applications, and ethics in planning practice, while engaging planners in real-world scenarios and best practices.

Grant Project Timeline & Summary

Project Summary

  • Summer 2024 — Launched project website; recruited and selected two research assistants and subject matter expert reviewers.
  • Fall 2024 — RAs conducted research and developed case study scenarios; began playtesting with APA New York and at NPC 2024 in Minneapolis.
  • Early 2025 — Finalized 72-card deck, Black Swan rules, and card images; completed editor review; printed cards for NPC 2025.
  • March 2025 — Featured session at NPC 2025 in Denver (~60 attendees, 1.5 CM credits).
  • Spring–Summer 2025 — Submitted reimbursements; continued handbook development; virtual playtesting with university students.
  • Fall 2025 — Playtested at APA Washington conference; launched game rules on APA GitHub; explored chapter distribution.
  • Late 2025–Early 2026 — Commissioned print run; resolved printing defects; published blog post on APA website.
